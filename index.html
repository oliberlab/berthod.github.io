Dr. Olivier Berthod

ICN Business School, Université de Lorraine
CEREFIGE, Nancy, France


I am an associate professor of organizational behavior at ICN Business School (Paris, Nancy, Berlin) and a faculty member of the CEREFIGE research institute at Université de Lorraine, Nancy, France. I do research on organization theory, decision-making, stakeholder engagement, and the pursuit of reliable, failure-free performances in businesses, public sector and nonprofit organizations. This is a static page listing some of my projects.


The design of cross-sector partnerships for environmental action

This project looks into cross-sector partnerships for climate actions and determines the level of organization needed for participants to perform on their goals.

Related results can be found here and others will be presented at the next colloquium of the European Group of Organization Studies.


Climate preparedness of cultural heritage institutions

Floods, fires, power outages or vandalism - cultural assets can be endangered or even destroyed by various events. This project attempts to create an overview of the perceived level of preparedness of cultural managers.

Our policy paper recently came out, to be found here.


Improving communication with patients

Funded by the Wagener Foundation (34.840 EUR)

We provided evidence supporting the Michael Scott effect in this paper and confirmed that attention to simpler wording does improve mutual understanding between patients and medical staff.


Developing and running high-reliability networks for large-scale events

Funded by the German Research Foundation (272.000 EUR)

In this project we

  • showed how crisis managers improvise with scarce resources in the face of unforeseen event in this paper,
  • and how they manage the expectations and sensitivities of their stakeholders while preparing for large, cultural and sporting events in this one,
  • provided a framework to study distributed settings here and here,
  • offered a definition of high-reliability networks in this article (a German translation can be found here)
  • and reported on a new approach to prepare for the unexpected in this and this papers.

Uncertainty and risks in the food industry

Funded by the Dahlem Research School (17.716 EUR)

In 2011, a food-borne disease outbreak spread in northern Germany. We reconstructed the emergence of the response network created by public managers here.


Failure-driven innovations

Funded by the Peter Pribilla Foundation (50.000 EUR)

We have studied why airplane pilots may not understand the unexpected algorithmic responses of their system and have published that discussion here. We also produced a small collection of case studies on failure-driven innovations, which you can find here.


Resistance to change and the management of World Heritage Sites

Funded by the German Research Foundation (PhD grant)

An in-depth case study of decision-makers torn between urban development and preservation of a World Heritage Site. Two chapters on this case have been published here and here.
