Current Projects

The design of cross-sector partnerships for environmental action

This project aims to determine the level of organization that cross-sector partnerships need to achieve their goals in environmental conservation.

First results will be presented at the next colloquium of the European Group of Organization Studies.


Disaster preparedness of cultural heritage institutions

Floods, fires, power outages or vandalism - cultural assets can be endangered or even destroyed by various events. This project attempts to create an overview of the needs for and methods of interorganizational and cross-sector emergency preparedness in practice.

Our policy paper recently came out, to be found here.



Completed Projects

Improving communication in socio-paediatric care

Funded by the Wagener Foundation (34.840 EUR)

We provided evidence supporting the Michael Scott effect in this paper and confirmed that attention to simpler wording does improve mutual understanding.


Developing and running high-reliability networks

Funded by the German Research Foundation (272.000 EUR)

We showed how to improvise with scarce resources in the face of unforeseen events here, how decision-makers manage the expectations and sensitivites of their stakeholder network between crises in this paper, provided a framework to study distributed settings here, offered a definition of high-reliability networks in this article (a German translation can be found here) and showed how to go beyond scenario planning in this and this papers.


Uncertainty and risks in the food industry

Funded by the Dahlem Research School (17.716 EUR)

In 2011, a food-borne disease outbreak spread over northern Germany. We reconstructed the emergence of the networked response created by public authorities here.


Failure-driven Innovations

Partly funded by the Peter Pribilla Foundation (50.000 EUR)

We have studied why airplane pilots may not understand the unexpected algorithmic responses of their system and have published that discussion here. We also produced a small collection of case studies on failure-driven innovations, which you can find here.


Resistance to change and the management of World Heritage sites

Funded by the German Research Foundation (PhD grant)

An in-depth case study of decision-makers torn between urban development and preservation of a World Heritage Site. Two chapters on this case have been published here and here.
