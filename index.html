I am an Associate Professor with the Department of Organizational Behavior at ICN Business School Paris, Nancy, Berlin, and a faculty member of the CEREFIGE research institute at Universite de Lorraine, Nancy, France. I hold master's degrees in management from the Universite Paris Dauphine-PSL, France, and the Linne Universitet in Vaxjo, Sweden, and a Doctorate rer. pol. from the Freie Universitat Berlin, Germany.

I do research on the organization of the State, questions of organizational design in general, and change issues. I have worked and published on topics such as organization theory, resistance to change, mindfulness, networks, high-reliability, decision-making, and stakeholder engagement in a variety of sectors and policy domains. This is a static, mobile-friendly webpage listing some of my current and past projects.

For a list of publications, please see my Google Scholar profile.


Organizing cross-sector collaborations

This project looks into cross-sector collaborations and determines the organizational processes required for participants to deliver sustainable change in the face of complex policy problems.

Related results can be found here and others were presented at the 2023 colloquium of the European Group of Organization Studies.


Risk and preparedness in cultural heritage institutions

With the support of the German National Academy of Sciences Leopoldine

Accidents, fires, power outages or vandalism - cultural assets can be endangered or even destroyed by various events. This project attempts to create an overview of the perceived level of preparedness and local embeddedness of cultural managers.

A policy paper has been published and can be consulted here.


Improving communication with patients

Funded by the Wagener Foundation (34.840 EUR)

We have provided evidence of the Michael Scott effect in this article and confirmed that attention to simpler wording improves mutual understanding between patients and medical staff.


Governing high-reliability networks for large-scale events

Funded by the German Research Foundation (272.000 EUR)

In this project we have

  • showed how crisis managers improvise with limited resources in the face of unforeseen events in this article (an earlier version won the best paper award at the EGOS conference in 2017),
  • and how they manage the expectations and sensitivities of their stakeholders while preparing for large-scale cultural and sporting events in this one (2017 best paper award from the scientific commission on public management, VHB, Germany),
  • provided a framework for studying distributed settings, available here and here,
  • offered a definition of high-reliability networks in this article (a German translation can be found here)
  • and presented a new approach to preparing for the unexpected in this article and this other one.

Uncertainty and risks in the food industry

Funded by the Dahlem Research School (17.716 EUR)

In 2011, an epidemic of food-borne illness spread across northern Germany. We have reconstructed the emergence of the response network created by public managers here (selection of the best papers from the scientific commission on organization's 2013 program, VHB, Germany).


Failure-driven innovations

Funded by the Peter Pribilla Foundation (50.000 EUR)

We have investigated why airplane pilots can misinterpret their system's algorithmic responses and published that discussion here. We have also produced a small collection of case studies on failure-based innovation, which you can read here.


Resistance to change and the management of World Heritage Sites

Funded by the German Research Foundation (PhD grant)

An in-depth case study of decision-makers torn between urban development and the preservation of a World Heritage site. Two chapters on this case have been published here and here.
