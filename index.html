Research

I am an Associate Professor at ICN Business School, France and Germany, and a member of the faculty at CEREFIGE, University of Lorraine, France. I do projects with businesses, public, and non profit organizations on behavior in and of organisations, change, collaboration, decision-making, communication and their impact on performance and effectiveness.

Current Projects

The design of cross-sector partnerships for environmental action

This project aims to determine the level of organization that partnerships need to achieve their goals in environmental conservation.

First results will be presented at the next colloquium of the European Group of Organization Studies.

Disaster preparedness of cultural heritage institutions

Floods, fires, power outages or vandalism - cultural assets can be endangered or even destroyed by various events. This project attempts to create an overview of the needs for and methods of interorganizational and cross-sector emergency preparedness in practice.

Our policy paper recently came out, to be found here.

Completed Projects

Improving communication in socio-paediatric care

Funded by the Wagener Foundation (34.840EUR)

We provided evidence supporting the Michael Scott effect in this paper and found out that an attention to simpler wording does improve mutual understanding.

Developing and running high-reliability networks

Funded by the German Research Foundation (272.000EUR)

We showed how to improvise with scarce resources in the face of unforeseen events here, how decision-makers manage the expectations and sensitivites of their stakeholder network in between crises in this one, provided a framework to study distributed settings there, offered a definition of high-reliability networks in this one (a German translation can be found here) and showed why scenarios are less helpful than we thought and where to go from there in this and this papers.

Uncertainty and risks in the food industry

Funded by the Dahlem Research School (17.716EUR)

In 2011, a food-borne disease outbreak wrought havoc on the north of Germany. We reconstructed this emergent process of network response there.

Making sense of unexpected algorithmic responses

Partly funded by the Peter Pribilla Foundation (50.000EUR)

We studied why airplane pilots can fail to make proper sense of unexpected algorithmic responses and crash and published this discussion there. We also produced a small collection of case studies on failure-driven innovations, which you can find there.

Resistance to change and the management of World Heritage sites

Funded by the German Research Foundation (PhD grant)

An in depth case study of decision makers torn between urban development and the preservation of a World Heritage site. Incidentally, two chapters related to this case were pubnlished here and there.
